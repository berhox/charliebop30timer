The Charter of Jondique

THE SUPREME LAW OF THE LAND

"We the Players of Jondique, in order to transition from Autocracy to Order, to ensure the rights of the Builder and the Miner, and to secure the stability of the Realm, do ordain and establish this Great Charter."

CHAPTER I: THE END OF AUTOCRACY

Article 1: The Abdication of Absolute Power

The Server Owner (Rybeez) hereby formally relinquishes the title and status of "Dictator." While retaining administrative ownership of the server files, Rybeez agrees that the power of /op shall no longer be used for personal enrichment, arbitrary punishment, or gameplay advantage.

The Server Owner submits to the jurisdiction of the Court and the provisions of this Charter.
Article 2: Separation of Powers

Governance shall be divided into three branches:

  • The General Assembly: The Legislative Voice of the Playerbase.
  • The Security Council: The Executive Enforcers of Server Integrity.
  • The High Court: The Judicial Arbiters of Disputes.

CHAPTER II: THE SECURITY COUNCIL

Article 3: Composition & The Executive Veto

The Security Council acts as the supreme executive body. It is comprised of the Server Owner and rotating 2 members from the General Assembly randomly chosen per 4 real days.

THE RYBEEZ CLAUSE: To ensure the ultimate stability of the realm, the Server Owner retains Absolute Veto Power over any legislation passed by the General Assembly.
Article 4: Powers of Non-Force (Soft Power)

The Council may utilize administrative pressure to enforce the law, including:

  • Economic Sanctions: Embargoes, trade blockades, and fines.
  • Asset Forfeiture: Freezing of bank accounts or claims suspected of duplication.
  • Civic Revocation: Stripping a player of voting rights or Statehood status.
Article 5: Powers of Force (Hard Power)

In cases of severe criminality or existential threat, the Council is authorized to use force including but not limited to:

  • Containment: Imprisonment in Bedrock or Obsidian facilities.
  • Exile: Temporary or Permanent Banning.

CHAPTER III: THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY

Article 6: Universal Suffrage

The General Assembly is the sovereign voice of the people. Every active PC Player on the whitelist holds exactly One Vote.

No title, rank, playtime, or wealth shall grant weighted voting power. The vote of the casual miner is equal to the vote of the wealthy industrialist.
Article 7: Legislative Competence

The Assembly holds the sole power to draft and pass legislation regarding:

  • Public Works: Construction of highways, nether hubs, and spawn amenities.
  • Statehood: The admission of new States and definition of borders.
  • Foreign Affairs: Declarations of War or Peace treaties with external entities.
  • Standardization: Establishing official currency and units of measurement.
Article 8: Parliamentary Procedure

To ensure order, the Assembly shall operate under the following rules:

  • The Motion: Any Member may propose a law. It must be "Seconded" by another Member to proceed to a vote.
  • Quorum: A vote is only valid if at least 67% of the active playerbase casts a ballot.
  • Filibuster: Debate on a motion may not exceed 24 hours before a mandatory vote is called.
Article 9: Voting Thresholds

Legislation requires specific majorities to pass:

  • Simple Resolution: 50% + 1 Vote (Standard laws, naming of roads).
  • Supermajority: 66% Vote (Constitutional Amendments, Impeachment of Judges).
  • Unanimous Consent: Required to override a Security Council Veto.

CHAPTER IV: STATEHOOD & TERRITORY

Article 10: Definition of a State

A State is a defined territory governed by a player or group, recognized by the General Assembly. To apply for Statehood, a territory must possess:

  • A designated Capital City with a Town Hall.
  • Clearly defined borders (Physical Walls or Coordinate Markers).
  • A distinct Banner/Flag.
Article 11: Privileges of Statehood

Upon admission by the Assembly, a State is granted:

  • Protection against Terraforming: Immunity from unauthorized external modification.
  • Representation: The right to fly their banner at Spawn.

CHAPTER V: RIGHTS & PRECEDENTS

Codified following the Supreme Court Rulings of 2025 (The Iron Trials).
Article 14: Due Process

No player shall be banned or stripped of property without a hearing before the Court, except in cases of immediate emergency (Active Griefing/Hacking).

THE CHARTER OF JONDIQUE
